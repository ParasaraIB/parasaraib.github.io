Maintaining your Arch Linux

Maintaining Arch Linux may be confusing at first especially for a begginer like you. Fear not, here I list several useful steps to make your Arch Linux becomes stable. Even for you, linux guru out there, you might find these tips useful. So without further ado, let's jump right into it!

DISCLAIMER: all risk are at your costs. Please do backup your important data before doing all these steps right away!!!

  • Clean Pacman package cache

    sudo pacman -Scc

  • Remove unused packages (orphans)

    sudo pacman -R $(pacman -Qtdq)

  • Clean cache in your /home

    rm -rf ~/.cache/*

  • Remove old configuration files

    Remove manually at ~/.local/share

  • Find broken symlinks

    sudo pacman -S rmlint

    sh rmlint.sh

  • Find files that takes up the most disk space

    sudo pacman -S ncdu

    ncdu

  • Use disk cleaning program (BleachBit)

    sudo pacman -S bleachbit


I hope that some of these tips will help you out through your journey in using Arch Linux. You can find out more of these stuffs at Average Linux User. He made a ton of linux-related videos which I found useful. Check it out! Happy exploring!
